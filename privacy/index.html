Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 February 2025

1. Introduction

Legacy Leaders ("we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting your personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you engage with our website and services.

We operate in accordance with the Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (Singapore) and applicable subsidiary legislation. By using our website or contacting us, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

Our services are directed exclusively at accredited investors as defined under the Securities and Futures Act (Cap. 289) and the Financial Advisers Act (Cap. 110). We do not knowingly collect personal data from individuals who do not meet this criterion.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect the following categories of personal data:

  • Identity data: Full name, date of birth, NRIC or passport number, and similar identifying information provided in the course of an engagement.
  • Contact data: Email address, telephone number, and residential or business address.
  • Financial data: Information about your assets, liabilities, income, and estate structure, provided for the purpose of estate planning advice.
  • Enquiry data: Information submitted through our contact form, estate assessment tool, or direct correspondence.
  • Technical data: IP address, browser type, and pages visited, collected automatically through standard web analytics tools.

Our estate assessment tool processes your responses locally in your browser. No assessment data is transmitted to or stored on our servers.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your enquiries and schedule consultations.
  • To provide estate planning, legal, and financial advisory services.
  • To prepare, draft, and review legal documents and financial plans on your behalf.
  • To communicate with you regarding your engagement, including updates and follow-up matters.
  • To comply with applicable legal and regulatory obligations, including those imposed by the Singapore Law Society and the Monetary Authority of Singapore.
  • To send periodic insights or updates, where you have consented to receive such communications.

We do not use your personal data for automated profiling, marketing to third parties, or any purpose inconsistent with those stated above.

4. Confidentiality and Professional Privilege

Information disclosed in the context of legal advisory services is protected by legal professional privilege to the extent applicable under Singapore law. Such information is held in strict confidence and will not be disclosed without your consent, except where required by law or court order.

All staff and third-party service providers engaged by Legacy Leaders are bound by confidentiality obligations consistent with the sensitivity of the information we handle.

5. Disclosure of Personal Data

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We may disclose your data to third parties only in the following circumstances:

  • With your consent: Where you have expressly authorised disclosure to specific parties (e.g., co-advisers, financial institutions, government bodies).
  • Legal or regulatory requirement: Where disclosure is required by law, court order, or regulatory authority, including the Singapore Law Society, MAS, or the Office of the Public Guardian.
  • Service providers: Trusted third parties who provide essential operational services (e.g., secure document management) and who are contractually bound to protect your data.

We do not disclose your data to insurance companies, financial product distributors, or any party that could use it for commercial solicitation without your explicit prior consent.

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, including to satisfy legal, accounting, and professional regulatory obligations.

For legal engagements, records are typically retained for a minimum of seven (7) years following the conclusion of the engagement, consistent with professional standards. After this period, data is securely deleted or anonymised.

7. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, destruction, or alteration. All data in transit is encrypted using industry-standard TLS protocols. Client documents are stored in access-controlled, encrypted environments.

Whilst we take all reasonable precautions, no transmission over the internet is entirely secure. If you have specific concerns about data security, please contact us to discuss alternative communication arrangements.

8. Your Rights

Under the PDPA, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data held by us.
  • Correct any inaccuracies in your personal data.
  • Withdraw consent to certain uses of your personal data, subject to legal and contractual limitations.
  • Request deletion of your personal data, subject to our legal retention obligations.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below. We will respond to all requests within 30 days.

9. Cookies and Analytics

Our website uses minimal analytics to understand how visitors interact with our content. No third-party advertising cookies are used. You may disable cookies in your browser settings; this will not affect your ability to use our website or contact us.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The date of the most recent revision is displayed at the top of this page. Continued use of our website or services following an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

Questions about your privacy?

If you have any questions, concerns, or requests regarding this Privacy Policy or the handling of your personal data, please contact us at privacy@legacyleaders.sg or through our contact form. We take all privacy matters seriously and will respond promptly.
